A Brief Tail of Origin

Domestic cats (Felis catus) are believed to have originated in the Middle East, specifically in the Fertile Crescent region of modern-day Iraq, from more than 12,000 years ago. It is believed that all domestic cats descended from a Middle Eastern wildcat, Felis sylvestris, which literally means "cat of the woods."

At that time, cats were attracted to human settlements because they were able to find food, specifically rodents that were attracted to stored grains, and became a useful presence for humans as they kept the rodent population under control, which helped to protect food stores and prevent the spread of disease.

Over time, cats became more domesticated and were viewed as a sacred animal and worshipped by some ancient cultures, such as the ancient Egyptians, who believed that cats possessed divine qualities, and they were often depicted in artwork and mythology.

Today, cats are one of the most popular pets in the world, with an estimated 600 million cats living in households worldwide.
